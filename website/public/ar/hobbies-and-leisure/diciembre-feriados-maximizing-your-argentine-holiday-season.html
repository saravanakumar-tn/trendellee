Diciembre Feriados: Maximizing Your Argentine Holiday Season

Dec 6, 2024 3 minutes read

¡Hola, amigos! December in Argentina? That means sun, family, friends…and lots of feriados (holidays)! If you're in Argentina this December, or planning a trip, knowing the holiday calendar is key to making the most of your time. Let's dive into what awaits.

So, what are the key December holidays in Argentina? While the exact dates change slightly each year, the core holidays remain. Keep an eye out for official announcements closer to the time for pinpoint accuracy, but typically expect celebrations around Christmas (25th December) and New Year's Eve (31st December). These are always national holidays. Expect businesses to be closed, and the atmosphere to be incredibly festive!

How to make the most of your December Feriados:

  • Embrace the asado culture: No December in Argentina is complete without a barbecue! Gather with friends and family, enjoy delicious carne, and soak up the convivial atmosphere.
  • Hit the beaches (if you're in the right place!): Many Argentinians head to the coast during the holiday season. The beaches of Mar del Plata, Cariló, or Pinamar are popular choices; book in advance!
  • Explore the mountains: For a different kind of escape, consider the stunning landscapes of Patagonia or the Andes. Hiking, skiing, and simply enjoying the breathtaking views are all on offer.
  • Enjoy the city life: Buenos Aires is buzzing during December. Take advantage of the festive spirit, visit the markets, see a show, or simply wander through the beautiful streets. Many museums may have special events as well.
  • Plan ahead!: Accommodation and flights fill up quickly during peak season. Book well in advance if you're planning a trip.

Beyond the beaches and barbecues:

Argentina's diverse landscape offers something for everyone. Consider exploring the wineries of Mendoza, hiking in Iguazu Falls National Park, or experiencing the unique culture of the northwest region. The possibilities are endless!

Remember, these feriados are a chance to truly experience Argentine culture and lifestyle. So, ¡disfruta! (Enjoy!)
