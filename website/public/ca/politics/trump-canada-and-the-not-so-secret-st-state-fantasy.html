Trump, Canada, and the (Not-So-Secret) 51st State Fantasy

Dec 5, 2024 5 minutes read

Let's be honest, the idea of Donald Trump and Canada sharing a particularly warm and fuzzy relationship is, shall we say, a bit of a stretch. Their interactions have been a rollercoaster of trade wars, pointed tweets, and enough awkward silences to fill a hockey rink. But the relationship goes beyond mere political sparring – it's seeped into the Canadian psyche in a way few other US presidents have managed.

The ‘51st state’ meme, for example, is a persistent undercurrent in the conversation. While never seriously proposed by any mainstream Canadian or American politician, the idea of Canada becoming part of the US continues to bubble up, particularly among those who find themselves on the opposite political spectrum to the current Canadian government. It’s a fascinating thought experiment, even if it remains firmly in the realm of fantasy (for now!).

Trump's presidency certainly fueled this fantastical idea. His ‘America First’ policies, coupled with his often-contentious approach to international trade, directly impacted Canada. The renegotiation of NAFTA (now USMCA), though ultimately successful, created considerable anxiety and uncertainty north of the border. Many Canadians felt Trump's actions displayed a distinct lack of respect for their country's sovereignty and economic interests. This perceived disrespect intensified the existing undercurrent of anxiety surrounding a powerful neighbor. It also fuelled political discourse in Canada about how to deal with its American neighbours.

Beyond the economic realm, Trump's personality and rhetoric have played a significant role. His bombastic style and willingness to engage in personal attacks haven’t exactly endeared him to many Canadians, who often value a more measured, less confrontational political discourse. Think of it this way: Canada's polite society is somewhat incompatible with Trump's blunt-force approach.

But the relationship isn't entirely defined by conflict. Despite the tensions, underlying economic ties and cultural exchanges remain strong. Millions of Canadians visit the US every year and vice versa. Many Canadian businesses depend on the US market, and American investment plays a significant role in the Canadian economy. Despite Trump’s policies, Canada remains a pivotal trade partner for the US. The fundamental strength of the existing relations has ultimately made it very difficult for Trump to actually break it down, despite the multiple attempts and the harsh rhetoric.

So, while the ‘51st state’ remains a playful (and often anxious) speculation, the reality is far more nuanced. The relationship between Canada and the US, under any president, is complex and deeply interconnected. Trump's presidency simply served to highlight some of the existing tensions and anxieties, making the ongoing dialogue about the future of this relationship even more pertinent.
